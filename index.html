About Me

I'm an MSc graduate in Network and Information Security from Kingston University London, with a strong foundation in cybersecurity, secure infrastructure, and risk assessment. I've worked on identifying technical vulnerabilities, understanding their business impact, and supporting security best practices across both cloud and on-prem environments. I approach problems with structure and clarity, always looking to balance deep technical understanding with practical application.

During my dissertation, I focused on the critical role of VPNs in maintaining secure communications, analyzing how different protocols (OpenVPN, IPSec, WireGuard) balance performance and security. My research culminated in the conceptual design of an Adaptive Protocol Selector System (APSS), aimed at dynamically selecting the optimal VPN protocol based on real-time network conditions.

I excel at communicating risks effectively, whether explaining a vulnerability to a stakeholder or discussing compliance needs with a team. I'm detail-oriented, proactive, and work best in environments where clarity, collaboration, and resilience matter, especially in high-pressure settings where secure systems make a real difference.

Enhancing VPN Performance using APSS

MSc. Dissertation Project

Addressed the trade-off between security and performance in VPNs by proposing an Adaptive Protocol Selector System (APSS).
